About

A Career Built on
Clinical Excellence

Dr Andreas Kyriacou is a Consultant Cardiologist and Cardiac Electrophysiologist at Sheffield Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust — one of the UK's leading tertiary cardiac centres. He holds dual appointments at the Sheffield Arrhythmia Centre and South Yorkshire Heart Centre, providing both NHS and private cardiac care across the region.

Internationally, Dr Kyriacou practises at the German Medical Institute (GMI) in Limassol, Cyprus, bringing world-class electrophysiology expertise to patients across Cyprus and the wider Eastern Mediterranean region.

His clinical focus encompasses the full spectrum of cardiac arrhythmia management, from complex catheter ablation procedures to advanced cardiac device implantation. He is recognised for his expertise in atrial fibrillation ablation, ventricular tachycardia ablation, and the management of patients with implantable electronic cardiac devices.

Expertise

Specialist Areas of
Clinical Practice

Catheter Ablation

Expert in complex ablation procedures including pulmonary vein isolation, left atrial flutter ablation, and advanced 3D electroanatomic mapping systems for precise lesion delivery.

Atrial Fibrillation (AF)

Comprehensive AF management including pharmacological rhythm control, electrical cardioversion, and catheter-based ablation for persistent, long-standing persistent, and paroxysmal AF.

Cardiac Devices

Full range of cardiac device implantation and follow-up: pacemakers, implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICD), cardiac resynchronisation therapy (CRT), and subcutaneous ICD systems.

EP Studies

Diagnostic electrophysiology studies to evaluate arrhythmia mechanisms, assess sinus and AV node function, and guide therapeutic decision-making in complex rhythm disorders.

Ventricular Tachycardia

Specialist in VT ablation including ischaemic VT, idiopathic VT, and substrate-based ablation in structural heart disease. Experience with epicardial and endocardial approaches.

Remote Monitoring

Comprehensive remote cardiac monitoring programme for patients with implanted devices, enabling proactive arrhythmia detection and timely clinical intervention without the need for frequent hospital visits.

Research

Academic &
Research Activity

Dr Kyriacou holds a PhD in Cardiac Electrophysiology and maintains an active programme of clinical research, with over 100 peer-reviewed publications and book chapters in international journals and textbooks.

His research interests span the mechanisms and catheter ablation of complex arrhythmias, cardiac device therapy and remote monitoring, risk stratification in heart failure, and the clinical applications of advanced mapping technologies.

He serves as a reviewer for leading cardiology and electrophysiology journals and has been an invited speaker at national and international cardiology meetings.
